About Me

    My name is Kyeongan (Karl) Kwon and I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Houston. I am also a member of the Computational Physiology Lab under Prof. Ioannis Pavlidis.

    I received my Master of Science in Computer Science from the University of Houston. I used to work for the i2C lab under Prof. Weidong (Larry) Shi on Mobile HCI and mobile security. I received my Bachelor of Science in Software Engineering from the Sejong University and working for the TmaxSoft and Dial Communications as a software engineer.

    My major area is focusing on information visualization and human-computer interaction (HCI). More specifically, I am interested in exploring how to visualize large scale and complex data effectively. I have created User Portrait, an online visualization tool to condense large scare data set. I have published several papers to ACM Ubicomp and IEEE VIS, and I have implemented the APIs. I also built Scholar Plot as a leading developer of our team. It is an online visualization tool to visualize scholarly research accomplishments in an insightful manner.
